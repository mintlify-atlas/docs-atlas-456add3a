---
title: "How Scorecard calculates its weighted aggregate score"
description: "How individual check scores from 0–10 combine into a weighted aggregate, what each risk-level weight means, and the limitations of aggregate scoring."
---

Scorecard evaluates each check independently and then combines the results into a single aggregate score. Understanding how scoring works helps you interpret results accurately and prioritize improvements.

## Individual check scores

Every check returns a score between 0 and 10, where 10 is the best possible result. Scores are not binary—many checks use partial scoring to reflect degrees of compliance. For example, the Branch-Protection check awards 3 out of 10 for preventing force pushes, 6 out of 10 for also requiring a reviewer, and so on up to 10 for the most comprehensive protection.

### Inconclusive scores

Some checks return `?` instead of a numeric score. This means the check was not applicable to the repository or could not be evaluated:

- **Not applicable:** For example, the Packaging check returns `?` if no releases are detected, since there is nothing to evaluate.
- **Inconclusive:** The Code-Review check returns `?` if all recent changes were made by bots, because bot activity alone does not provide enough signal to assess human review practices.

A `?` score is excluded from the aggregate calculation—it neither helps nor hurts the overall score.

## Aggregate score calculation

The aggregate score is a **weighted average** of all individual check scores, where each check's weight is determined by its risk level:

| Risk level | Weight |
|---|---|
| Critical | 10 |
| High | 7.5 |
| Medium | 5 |
| Low | 2.5 |

The formula is:

```
aggregate = sum(score_i × weight_i) / sum(weight_i)
```

Where the sums are taken over all checks that returned a numeric score (checks that returned `?` are excluded from both the numerator and denominator).

### Example

Consider a project with these results:

| Check | Risk | Weight | Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dangerous-Workflow | Critical | 10 | 10 |
| Binary-Artifacts | High | 7.5 | 10 |
| Maintained | High | 7.5 | 1 |
| Vulnerabilities | High | 7.5 | 10 |
| Pinned-Dependencies | Medium | 5 | 8 |
| Security-Policy | Medium | 5 | 0 |
| License | Low | 2.5 | 10 |
| Packaging | Medium | 5 | ? *(excluded)* |

Weighted sum of scores: `(10×10) + (10×7.5) + (1×7.5) + (10×7.5) + (8×5) + (0×5) + (10×2.5)` = `100 + 75 + 7.5 + 75 + 40 + 0 + 25` = `322.5`

Sum of weights (excluding `?`): `10 + 7.5 + 7.5 + 7.5 + 5 + 5 + 2.5` = `45`

Aggregate score: `322.5 / 45` ≈ **7.2 / 10**

## What the aggregate score does and does not tell you

The aggregate score provides a single number that summarizes a project's security posture, but it has important limitations:

**The aggregate score does not tell you what specific behaviors a project is or is not following.** Multiple combinations of individual check scores can produce the same aggregate. A project with a 7.0 aggregate might have perfect scores on most checks but a critical gap in one area, or it might have mediocre scores across the board.

**Risk weighting reflects relative importance, not absolute severity.** A Critical-risk check scored at 0 pulls the aggregate down more than a Low-risk check scored at 0, but neither tells you how likely a specific attack is.

**Scores change over time as heuristics are refined.** Scorecard's checks are heuristics—they are updated as the project learns about new attack patterns, improves detection accuracy, and adds new checks. A project's aggregate score may change between scans even if the project itself has not changed, because the scoring logic was updated.

## Limitations and false positives

- **False positives:** A check may flag an issue that does not actually represent a risk for a specific project. For example, the Contributors check may score a single-maintainer project at 0, even though that is an intentional project structure rather than a security gap.
- **False negatives:** A project may follow good security practices that Scorecard cannot automatically detect. For example, a project using a CI system that Scorecard does not recognize will score low on CI-Tests even if it runs comprehensive tests.
- **Not a definitive security assessment:** Scorecard is an automated tool designed to surface common heuristics quickly. It is not a substitute for a manual security audit, threat modeling, or penetration testing.

<Tip>
  Focus on individual check scores and their remediation steps rather than optimizing for the aggregate. Addressing a Critical-risk check scored at 0 provides far more real security benefit than improving several Low-risk checks from 8 to 10.
</Tip>

## Checks excluded from weekly scan

The [Scorecard REST API](/usage/rest-api) publishes results from weekly scans of the top 1 million open source projects. Three checks are omitted from these scans due to the API costs of running them at scale:

- CI-Tests
- Contributors
- Dependency-Update-Tool

These checks are still run when you use the [Scorecard CLI](/usage/cli) or [GitHub Action](/usage/github-action) directly.
